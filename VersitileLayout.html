Washington, DC, the U.S. capital, is a compact city on the Potomac River, bordering the states of Maryland and Virginia. It’s defined by imposing neoclassical monuments and buildings – including the iconic ones that house the federal government’s 3 branches: the Capitol, White House and Supreme Court. It's also home to iconic museums and performing-arts venues such as the Kennedy Center. ― Google

While Washington D.C. is known for its many historical monuments, there are other activities to do in the area that do not revolve around historical sight seeing. There are plentiful opportunities to see shows and live music in the area, as well as retuaraunts and bars to enjoy the night life.

To navigate your war around D.C., many people use the metro to get from place to place. In addition to this, bikes can come in handy when attempting to avoid typical D.C. traffic.
